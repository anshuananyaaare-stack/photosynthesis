<section>
    <h2>Introduction</h2>
    <p>
        Photosynthesis is a vital biological process by which green plants
        prepare their own food using sunlight. This process is the foundation
        of life on Earth, as it provides food and oxygen for living organisms.
    </p>
</section>

<section>
    <h2>Definition</h2>
    <p>
        Photosynthesis is the process in which green plants use carbon dioxide,
        water, and sunlight to synthesize food (glucose) in the presence of
        chlorophyll and release oxygen as a by-product.
    </p>
</section>

<section>
    <h2>Raw Materials Required</h2>
    <ul>
        <li>Sunlight</li>
        <li>Water</li>
        <li>Carbon Dioxide</li>
        <li>Chlorophyll</li>
    </ul>
</section>

<section>
    <h2>Process of Photosynthesis</h2>
    <p>
        The process of photosynthesis occurs mainly in the leaves of plants.
        Leaves contain chloroplasts which have chlorophyll. Chlorophyll traps
        sunlight. Water absorbed by roots reaches the leaves through xylem,
        while carbon dioxide enters the leaves through stomata.
    </p>
    <p>
        Using light energy, water and carbon dioxide are converted into glucose.
        Oxygen is released into the atmosphere.
    </p>
</section>

<section>
    <h2>Chemical Equation</h2>
    <p>
        6CO<sub>2</sub> + 6H<sub>2</sub>O + Sunlight →
        C<sub>6</sub>H<sub>12</sub>O<sub>6</sub> + 6O<sub>2</sub>
    </p>
</section>

<section>
    <h2>Importance of Photosynthesis</h2>
    <ul>
        <li>Provides food for plants and animals</li>
        <li>Maintains oxygen balance in the atmosphere</li>
        <li>Controls carbon dioxide levels</li>
        <li>Supports all life forms directly or indirectly</li>
    </ul>
</section>

<section>
    <h2>Conclusion</h2>
    <p>
        Photosynthesis is essential for the survival of life on Earth.
        Without this process, there would be no food, no oxygen, and no
        balance in nature.
    </p>
</section>
